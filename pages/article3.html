Preparation for Senior High

Just after high-school or grade 10, it is supposed that the students had already been prepared to discern whatever course or track they will take for the next chapter of their life, which is the senior high. As students, guidance and careful preparation are needed for them to have enough knowledge and wisdom to make the right decision.


Last August 14-18, 2017, we had our Career Guidance week, which was held inside VBA’s auditorium, this was attended by the grades 9 and 10. Through this activity they can at least have a glimpse about what track they would take up.


There were two speakers the academy has invited to speak about career guidance, one of which was Pastor Abner Canadido; he spoke about having the right mind and heart about the decision of choosing a career. He mentioned that in order to stay in one track- without any shifts, you have to pray earnestly and to examine yourself on where God is calling you. Parents, guardians, teachers, and pastors are great opportunity for you to get ideas and insights from. Believing that they can be very supportive, helpful, and generous in giving some advice. In spite of these, God’s calling should be obeyed . Where he wants you to be, you should follow.


The second speaker was from AMA computer college’s staff, Sir Glenn Rocili. He shared his knowledge about the K-12 curriculum and later explained each of the strands that can be taken up in senior high. Throughout the discussion, he confidently suggested the students to take up I.T. or Information Technology, for it will surely help and improve their skills about technology. Surely, this will be used in our generation today, for our technology is rapidly becoming more high-tech. As Sir Glenn shared many more about his knowledge about careers, he mainly focused on telling the students to keep doing their best especially in developing their given talents and skills, so that they will be wiser in their career choices. When they arrive at making decisions may their choice be aligned with what God wants them to do in their life.


by Ryan Roger R. Ramos
