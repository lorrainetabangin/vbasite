VBA’s Organizational Structure


Victory Baptist Academy, located at #56 Palma, First Road, Urbano Brgy., Baguio City continues to be one of the many schools using A.C.E. curriculum in Baguio City. It’s no wonder that the staff in this academy are Christians. We spare some time recognizing each of them- their roles and positions in the academy considering their contributions to the school, their effort and willingness to be used by God.


Pastor Basilio Silverio Widwidan Jr. is the newly elected pastor of Victory Baptist Church.. We now look up to him as the new administrator of the school as well. He is called by God to preach His Word. In June of this year, he will be formally shepherding VBC, the church where he grew up. He was born and raised in the city of Baguio and a graduate of Saint Louis University. He has been a missionary to the neglected, forgotten and despised people in prison, specifically to the male inmates at the Maximum Security Prison in Muntinlupa. He has also been actively participating in various rehabilitation and restoration programs related to alcoholism and drug abuse.


The second person whom we look up to is our energetic principal, Ms. Nancy Culay-an. She has been with Victory Baptist Academy since its start. Simply saying she’s been with the academy program for more than 20 years already. Considering her length of service to VBA, she serves as an effective liaison officer with staff, parents, students, and the community. She also guides the daily detail of school operation. Furthermore she works with the staff on curriculum development and represents the school in any interschool officers’ meeting. If there are some needs to be addressed she spearhead the staff in strategising and creating some viable solution to any concern.


Prior to our current secretary Ma’am Marlyn Leon was Ma’am Merly Raymundo. In any academy the role of a secretary is indispensable. Without her school records will be disorganized. The various role of a secretary includes the following: receiving calls, preparing letter communication, releasing students’ or staff’s slip, a go-between the principal and parents, arranging of principal’s schedule, etc. Her job isn’t limited inside the school premise as there are things that need to be done outside like: representing the school for any meeting, doing some bank errands and purchasing school materials as needed.


Ma’am Jebrah A. Leon is gifted by God in the area of Music. It isn’t surprising that she handles her music class very well. In fact her former students in Music now have their own students to train and are currently handling instrumental lessons. They also have their outreach- teaching instruments to children at Victory Baptist Mission in Rabon, San Fabian, Pangasinan. Her ability doesn’t stop there because she’s also handling Math for the elementary and high school students. So who says that Math and Music don’t go together?


Next, the Grade 9 supervisor, Sir Agustin Leon, is the loving husband of Ma’am Jebrah Leon, and a caring father to their three children. He sometimes speaks in our chapel hour every Wednesday morning. Having a good sense of humor, we can consider him as one of the most cheerful teachers of VBA. Never is a dull moment whenever he speaks in chapel hour. While he often makes his student laugh, dealing with high school students is not an easy task. Yet he never tires in giving them fatherly advice at the same time admonishing them about their need of a loving Savior.


If there is one favorite among the mini classes, it’s no other than the P.E. class. All because of the “sporty” teacher of VBA, Sir Dennis Jardinez. He is the P.E. teacher for all levels. Although P.E. is a popular subject to most students, you can not underestimate the discipline Sir. Jardinez is giving the students. It isn’t surprising that some students are scared of him. By the way , we are looking forward to the most awaited event in his life- his wedding day! We earnestly wish him and his future wife, Elyzel all the best.


One of the male staff of VBA is Sir Jim Steven Espelico, the brother of Sir Jefrey Espelico. He shares the responsibility of handling the Grades 7-8 with Sir Jardinez. When it comes to computer operation and problems, he’s an expert you can count on. He also takes turn with other staff in preaching during chapel hours sharing his experiences and lessons in life. His inclination to art is a big help to the students especially during the NSC and JSC. All because he generously mentors the students for their entries.


The ever supportive teacher of the Grade 6 is Ma’am Mary Jane Cigaral. She enjoys handling her EPP and Art classes. While she considers her class with the elementary challenging, she considers teaching the Gr. 9 and 10 more challenging. She finds special fulfillment in teaching students and would be glad to keep on teaching as long as she has the chance. She believes that God’s Word should be given priority in educating the students. She is strict when it comes to learning center discipline.


Sir Jefrey Angelo Espelico is the supervisor of the Grade 5 pupils. In addition he takes care of the Art class for Grade 5 and also the Asian Literature class of the high-school students. VBA has been consistently garnering awards in the Art category of the JSC because of his able mentorship. He willingly, diligently and patiently train students and pupils to come up with highly competitive entries .Sir Espelico’s charisma towards the student are evident as they can easily shower him with attention. Albeit he can also be assertive when it comes to teaching and disciplining his pupils.


Sir Warlito Obidoza, another staff of VBA serves as the supervisor of the Grades 3-4. He also handles Music and EPP classes in the elementary. Some of his pupils treats him as their father. In fact Sir Obidoza has difficult time sending them to their line after the break and lunch time. They always like to crowd around him. Being a former pastor, he speaks with confidence during chapel hours. He may be a strict teacher but he can also be a tender-hearted father to his pupils.


Next is the ever consistent supervisor of the Grade 2 pupils- Ma’am Marcy Maglanque. She diligently and patiently assists her students in their classroom activities and sees to it that they finish their goals or else it would mean they have to do over time. Of course we won’t forget about her cooking prowess with her high school TLE class. In fact after this school year, the high school students under her will surely be good cook in their homes.


The VBA staff will not be complete without Ma’am Rowena Baniaga. Her energetic pupils settle down every time they sit with her in their values class. She patiently teaches them Bible stories and inspiring songs for children. Her ability to play the guitar surely aids in catching their enthusiasm in class. David who used to be impatient with his PACEs can now sit down and without complaint accomplish his PACEs. Surely Ma’am Rowena’s strategy in dealing with him has been working well.


Many a times students and pupils can’t wait for lunch time to come. That’s because of the irresistible, delicious smelling food coming from the kitchen. Blame this on the kitchen staff who works hard to come up with delicious and nutritious meals everyday. Thank you Maam Lisa Melchor, Maam Carol Calaunan and Ma’am Anna Montano. Thank you most of all for safeguarding our health by generously serving food to the academy with your caring and hardworking hands. No wonder some people say students who stay in VBA usually gain weight.


Every morning when we arrive in school, we are greeted by our clean learning centers including our school surroundings. Thank you Sir Gener Leon for keeping our learning centers spic and span. We can focus more on our goals because of our clean school environment. We would be glad to work and cooperate with you to have a clean school suitable for learning.
